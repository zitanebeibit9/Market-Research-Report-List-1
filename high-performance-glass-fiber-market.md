Polymer composites utilize glass fibers combined with resins or polymers, enhancing strength and durability, making them ideal for applications in automotive and aerospace sectors. Non-polymer composites, on the other hand, include applications in cement, concrete, and other inorganic materials, providing increased tensile strength and impact resistance. Both types cater to industries requiring lightweight, high-strength materials that can withstand extreme conditions.</p></p>
